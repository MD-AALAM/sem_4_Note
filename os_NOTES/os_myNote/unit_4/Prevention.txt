🔒 Deadlock Prevention in Operating System
📌 Definition

->Deadlock Prevention is a technique in which the system ensures that at least one of the four necessary conditions of deadlock never occurs.

👉 Since deadlock occurs only when all four conditions are present simultaneously,
if we break even one condition, deadlock cannot occur.

📌 Important Theory Line for Exam

->Deadlock prevention works by restraining the way requests for resources can be made, so that at least one of the necessary conditions for deadlock is violated.

🔥 Advantages

✔ Guarantees no deadlock
✔ Simple concept

❌ Disadvantages

✖ Low CPU utilization
✖ Poor resource utilization
✖ May cause starvation
✖ Not practical for real systems
