1️⃣ What are Addressing Modes?
📌 Definition:

Addressing Modes are the different ways in which an instruction specifies the location of its operand (data).

In simple words:

👉 Addressing mode tells the CPU where to find the data required for execution.

These modes are interpreted by the Control Unit of the Central Processing Unit during the Decode phase of the instruction cycle.

2️⃣ Why Are Addressing Modes Needed?

Addressing modes help in:

✔ Reducing instruction size
✔ Increasing flexibility
✔ Improving execution efficiency
✔ Supporting complex data structures
